# What is Cloud Computing?

## Simple Definition

**Cloud Computing** means **using computing resources (servers, storage, databases, networking, etc.) over the internet instead of owning physical hardware.**

---

## Interview Answer

> "Cloud computing is the delivery of computing services like servers, storage, databases, and networking over the internet. Instead of buying and maintaining physical servers, we rent these resources from cloud providers like AWS and pay only for what we use."

## Real-life Example

🏠 **Electricity Analogy**

- You **don't build your own power plant**.
- You use electricity when needed and pay for what you consume.

Similarly,

- You **don't buy servers**.
- You use AWS servers over the internet and pay only for what you use.

---

## Key Points

- ✅ Resources are available over the internet.
- ✅ No need to buy physical servers.
- ✅ Pay only for what you use (Pay-as-you-go).
- ✅ Easy to scale up or down.

---

## AWS Connection

AWS is a **Cloud Service Provider (CSP)** that offers cloud computing services such as:

- **EC2** → Virtual Servers
- **S3** → Storage
- **RDS** → Databases
- **VPC** → Networking

---

## Interview Tip

**Q: Why do companies use cloud computing?**

**Answer:**

- Saves cost (no hardware purchase)
- Easily scalable
- Highly available
- Faster deployment
- Managed by cloud providers like AWS

### One-line answer to remember:

> **Cloud computing is renting IT resources over the internet instead of buying and maintaining physical hardware.**
